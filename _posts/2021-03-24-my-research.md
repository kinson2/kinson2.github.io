# My Research
As a teaching assistant professor, I have a unique opportunity to conduct research in a mixture of frameworks. The mixture I choose is one part statistical methodology with a splash of statistics education and pedagogy.

## Statistical Methodology Research

My statistical methodology research interests are dimension reduction techniques for normal and non-normal data, applied multivariate methods, and statistical graphics. You can find my scholarly activity below.

- Paper: Christopher Kinson, Xiwei Tang, Zhen Zuo & Annie Qu (2020) Longitudinal Principal Component Analysis With an Application to Marketing Data, Journal of Computational and Graphical Statistics, 29:2, 335-350, DOI: 10.1080/10618600.2019.1677244  https://doi.org/10.1080/10618600.2019.1677244

## Statistics Education and Pedagogy Research

My statistics education and pedagogy research interests center on technologically-enhanced learning, student engagement, and agency inside of the classroom. Specific methods of interest are continuous management systems, paired programming, reproducibility in the course workflow and classroom, and POC-student placemaking in the statistics discipline. I strongly believe in increasing efforts to recruit, retain, and sustain underrepresented students in STEM. You can find my scholarly activity below.

- Presentation: Christopher Kinson and David Dalpiaz (2021) Using GitHub in the Classroom: A VERZUZ Battle, United States Conference on Teaching Statistics. https://www.causeweb.org/cause/uscots/uscots21/program/breakouts 

- Poster: Christopher Kinson and David Unger (2019) Real DataSETs: Student Engagement Techniques for the Advanced Data Analysis Course, United States Conference on Teaching Statistics, P1-11. https://www.causeweb.org/cause/uscots/uscots19/posters/1-11

- Presentation: Christopher Kinson (2018) Learning from Dr. Martin Luther King Jr: Text analysis and statistical approaches for civil rights, Conference on Statistical Learning and Data Science. https://publish.illinois.edu/sldsc2018/2018/05/20/session-36-advances-in-nonparametric-statistics-and-their-applications/
